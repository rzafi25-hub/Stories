LeBron James turns heads in custom Blue Jackets jersey at LA Lakers game

By Jackie Longo

LeBron James turned heads at Wednesday night's Los Angeles Lakers game against the Denver Nuggets, and it wasn't due to his performance on the basketball court.

The four-time NBA Most Valuable Player, currently out with a groin injury, showed up to Crypto.com Arena in Los Angeles, rocking a custom NHL Columbus Blue Jackets jersey. The 40-year-old NBA star, who grew up in Akron, Ohio - about two hours north of Columbus - accessorized the blue and red jersey with a hat worn backward and two silver necklaces.

While fans tried to focus on the game, they couldn't help but comment on the NBA player's fashion choice.

One person commented on a video of the fit shared by ESPN on X, "LeBron's jersey collab - ice-cold heat."

Another person wrote, "This might actually make LeBron the goat. What a power move."

Someone else commented, "the collab we never knew we needed."

Another person said, "That jersey is [fire emojis]."

Even the Blue Jackets commented on the look.

"No debate: LeBron is the [goat emoji]," the NHL team wrote in a post on X.

The Blue Jackets are no stranger to the arena the Lakers played in last night. The team has split their series in the 2024-25 NHL regular season with the Lakers' co-tenants, the Los Angeles Kings.

The Columbus team is currently on a four-time losing streak. They face the reigning Stanley Cup champions, the Florida Panthers, at Nationwide Arena in Columbus on Thursday.

While the Blue Jackets haven't had the most luck recently, the Lakers won their third game in a row on Wednesday with a final score of 120-108.

When will James return to the Lakers?


According to ESPN reporter Shams Charania, James had his first on-court workout since suffering a groin injury on March 16 but isn't expected to play in another game for at least a week.

Due to the fact that he's suffering from a soft tissue injury, the Lakers want to take extra caution and not rush his return to the court, Charania said.

James was injured in the matchup against the Boston Celtics on March 8 and has missed the Lakers' last six games.

After the game, he told reporters he possibly extended it a little bit too much when he was trying to move past Celtics guard Jaylen Brown on a scoring attempt near the end of the fourth quarter.

He had 14 rebounds, 22 points and nine assists in his 35 minutes of play in the matchup against the Celtics before he suffered the injury to his left groin.
